* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * !!! IMPORTANT !! RULES CHANGES & CLARIFICATIONS !!! * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>RULEBOOK <C> These rules updates apply to the "current" Dark Age II rulebook, which is version 2.9, dated 22nd March 2002.<L.31.1>
There is also a version 2.10 rulebook, dated 18th March 2004, that already incorporates these changes, but at present it's<L.31.1>
available only in the online formats.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>MAINTENANCE <C> In DA74 onwards households and reserves are included in maintenance costs under the normal rules (each<L.31.1>
separately as if they're located in an area with a population of five). If you can't pay your maintenance costs then you disband<L.31.1>
reserves and household troops instead (each army disbanded pays a 1 BP cost). Troops are removed from the biggest first. Troops<L.31.1>
on the map are not affected: once they're in play they stay that way.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>VPS & END GAME <C> In DA74 onwards the VP penalty for losing an area is now equal to the population (but always at least 1<L.31.1>
VP). When there's a win pending the next census turn is double length and counts for double points. The Gaelic kingoms (Picts,<L.31.1>
Scots and Irish) are now included in the plunder rule (5.11) the same as the vikings (except they get army reserves, not ships).<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>TAX <C> The TAX action now raises 2 BPs instead of one.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>SABOTAGE <C> I've changed the SABOTAGE action so that instead of destroying an army it merely disperses it back into reserve.<L.31.1>
At the point of the action it's effectively doing the same thing as always, but this way makes a bit more sense.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>BUILD ACTION <C> I've changed the BUILD action to allow the ALL and ALL BUT formats.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>BATTLES <C> A proportion of the armies lost in each land battle are now dispersed instead of being destroyed. They're added<L.31.1>
back to your army reserve at the end of the turn (at the same time as other dispersals).<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
<Z>LOAN TROOPS <C> Kingdoms may loan troops to each other, from the reserve of one kingdom to the reserve of the other. They<L.31.1>
can't be used until the following turn. A kingdom may loan troops to ONE other kingdom, and may borrow troops from ONE other<L.31.1>
kingdom (but you are allowed to borrow from one kingdom and loan to a different one). Troops that have been loaned to you can<L.31.1>
be returned any time you like. You can recall troops that you've loaned to someone else whenever you like, provided the other<L.31.1>
kingdom has troops in their reserve. Troops returned from loans can't be used until the following turn.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
The BORROW action allows you say which other kingdom you're willing to borrow troops from. The LOAN action sends troops to<L.31.1>
another kingdom (provided they previously made a BORROW action to say that you're allowed to). You can't send loan troops to<L.31.1>
someone who hasn't made an action to say they want them.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
The RETURN action sends borrowed troops back to the kingdom they came from (they come from your reserve, and you can do this at<L.31.1>
any time - but you can't return loan troops to a non-player kingdom). The RECALL action takes loaned troops back from the<L.31.1>
reserve of the kingdom they came from (you can do this at any time).<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
The usual sequence is the kingdom that wants to borrow troops makes a BORROW action. The other kingdom can then make a LOAN<L.31.1>
action (later in the same turn, or in a later turn). Multiple LOAN actions are allowed. Either side can end the loan, even at an<L.31.1>
inconvenient moment.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>

from, or who to RETURN them to, so you don't need to say WHO on RECALL or RETURN actions.<L.31.1>
