<Z>Playoff Keys and Bonuses <C> I'm considering making a change to way keys and bonuses work in the playoffs. During the regular<L.31.1>
season, you have one key and one bonus per opponent, which seems to me to be perfectly logical. Once you get into the playoffs,<L.31.1>
the decision making process gets rather simpler - you have the same opponent for all (two or) three series so many coaches apply<L.31.1>
the same bonus and key for each series. Combined with the fact that you don't have injuries to contend with (which I don't propose<L.31.1>
to change) and probably not fatigue either it's all rather automatic, and gives you rather fewer decisions to make.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
My suggestion is that you should still have three keys for the playoffs, but that all three keys will apply for every game (on a<L.31.1>
sliding scale). It'll give you more decisions to make, and is probably more realistic too. During the regular season teams play<L.31.1>
one opponent for three days, then move on, and there aren't too many opportunites for your coaching staff to make specific<L.31.1>
adjustments for particular opponents (which is what keys and bonuses are meant to reflect). However, during the playoffs a team<L.31.1>
have 7-10 days against one particular opponent, so it's rather more logical that they'll have more time to hatch specific plans.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
At the same time, I may well crank up the effectiveness of keys and bonuses overall, to make them a little more significant.<L.31.1>

it rather more viable to key opposition pitchers, because there's much less worry about keying a pitcher who then doesn't play<L.31.1>
