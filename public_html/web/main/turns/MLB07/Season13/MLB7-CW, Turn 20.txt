<Z>Playoff Keys and Bonuses <C> I'm still keen for feedback on the possibility of making a change to way keys and bonuses work in<L.31.1>
the playoffs. Thus far I've not had much, and it may be a case of "if it ain't broke, don't fix it".<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
During the regular season, you have one key player and one bonus player per opponent, plus one bonus skill (which applies to the<L.31.1>
whole team) which seems to me to be perfectly logical. Once you get into the playoffs, the decision making process gets rather<L.31.1>
simpler - you have the same opponent for all (two or) three series so many coaches apply the same bonuses and key for each series<L.31.1>
(though the bonus skill varies more often, based on the stadium type). Combined with the fact that you don't have injuries to<L.31.1>
contend with (which I don't propose to change) and probably not fatigue either it's all rather automatic, and gives you rather<L.31.1>
fewer decisions to make.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
My suggestion is that you should still have three player keys for the playoffs, but that all three keys will apply for every game<L.31.1>
(on a sliding scale, so the first key is more effective than the second, and that more effective than the third). The same would<L.31.1>
apply with the bonus player, but not the bonus skill (so you could still "neutralise" a key with a bonus if you choose - a player<L.31.1>
bonused by you and keyed by your opponent plays "as normal", but obviously your bonus is used up). It'll give you more decisions<L.31.1>
to make, and is probably more realistic too. During the regular season teams play one opponent for three days, then move on, and<L.31.1>
there aren't too many opportunites for your coaching staff to make specific adjustments for particular opponents (which is what<L.31.1>
keys and bonuses are meant to reflect). However, during the playoffs a team have 7-10 days against one particular opponent, so<L.31.1>
it's rather more logical that they'll have more time to hatch specific plans.<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
At the same time, I could also crank up the effectiveness of keys and bonuses overall, to make them a little more significant, but<L.31.1>
that's really a separate issue. So basically there are three suggestions, the second of which I don't think I like already!<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
1. Should the three key players and three bonus players apply for all games, but with sliding effectiveness?<L.31.1>
2. Should the bonus skills apply for all games?<L.31.1>
3. Should the overall effectiveness of key players and bonus players be cranked up?<L.31.1>
<L.12.1>
Comments are welcome - I think no.1 will be more realistic, as well as giving you more decisions points to make (it'd also make it<L.31.1>
rather more viable to key opposition pitchers, because there's much less worry about keying a pitcher who then doesn't play<L.31.1>
because your opponent has juggled his rotation) and more control over what can happen in the playoffs.<L.31.1>
